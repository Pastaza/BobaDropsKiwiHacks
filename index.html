⛅ Amazing Cloud Facts

💧

Clouds Weigh a Lot!

A typical cumulus cloud weighs about 1.1 million pounds (500,000 kg) — that's like 100 elephants floating in the sky!

🌡️

Clouds Can Be Super Cold

High-altitude cirrus clouds form at temperatures as low as -40 °C (-40 °F) and are made of tiny ice crystals.

🏔️

Clouds Live High Up

Noctilucent clouds form at the edge of space, around 80 km (50 miles) above Earth's surface!

⚡

Lightning Factories

A single cumulonimbus can reach 18 km (11 miles) high and generate 100+ lightning bolts per minute.

🌈

Rainbow Makers

Water droplets in clouds act as tiny prisms that split sunlight into all colours of the rainbow.

🌍

Clouds Cover Earth

At any given moment, about 67 % of Earth's surface is covered by clouds — they play a huge role in our climate!

🔬

Tiny Droplets

Clouds are made of water droplets so tiny it takes about 1 million of them to make a single raindrop.

🪐

Clouds on Other Planets

Venus has clouds of sulfuric acid, while Jupiter's famous storms are giant cloud systems bigger than Earth!

🎨

Clouds Change Colour

Clouds appear white, grey, pink, orange, and even green before tornadoes depending on light and storm conditions.

🌤️ Types of Clouds

Cumulus

Puffy, white "fair-weather" clouds that look like cotton balls. Found at low to mid altitudes.

Cirrus

Thin, wispy, feather-like clouds at high altitudes, made entirely of ice crystals.

Stratus

Flat, grey blanket clouds that cover the whole sky, bringing drizzle and overcast days.

Cumulonimbus

The mighty thunderstorm cloud! Towering, dark, and packed with lightning and heavy rain.

Altocumulus

Mid-level fluffy clouds that appear in rows or waves, sometimes called a "mackerel sky."

Nimbostratus

Thick, dark grey clouds that bring steady, continuous rainfall or snowfall.
